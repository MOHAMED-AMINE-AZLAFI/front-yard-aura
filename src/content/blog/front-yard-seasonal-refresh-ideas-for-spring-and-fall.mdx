A seasonal refresh can make a front yard feel alive again without a major redesign. Spring and fall are especially powerful because they reset the mood of the home.

The best refreshes are simple. Clean the beds, prune what needs shaping, add fresh mulch, and update a few key areas with seasonal color.

You do not need to change the entire yard. Focus on the places people notice first.

## Start With Cleanup

Seasonal color looks better when the yard is already clean. Remove dead stems, rake leaves, prune overgrown shrubs, and redefine bed edges.

This creates a fresh background for flowers, planters, and mulch.

In spring, focus on winter damage and early weeds. In fall, focus on leaves, tired annuals, and preparing beds for cooler weather.

### Refresh Mulch

Mulch is one of the easiest ways to make a yard look renewed. A light refresh can deepen color, cover bare spots, and make plants stand out.

Do not add too much. Rake the existing mulch first and add only what is needed for an even surface.

## Update The Planters

Planters are the fastest way to show the season. In spring, use fresh greens and cool flowers. In fall, use richer tones, ornamental cabbage, grasses, or simple evergreen arrangements.

Keep containers connected to the home’s color palette. Seasonal does not have to mean loud.

## Add Color Where It Counts

Do not spread seasonal flowers everywhere. Concentrate them near the front door, walkway, or a main flower bed.

Focused color looks more intentional and is easier to maintain.

## Conclusion

A seasonal front yard refresh works because it makes the home feel current and cared for. Clean first, refresh mulch, update planters, and use color in the places people notice most.

Small seasonal changes can create a big emotional shift from the street.
